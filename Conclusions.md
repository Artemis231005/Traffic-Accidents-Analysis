# Conclusions obtained from Exploratory Data Analaysis (EDA)

This project analyzed traffic accident data using visualization techniques to identify key patterns and trends affecting safety on roads. It explores data during the 2021-2023 period. 

The analysis revealed that fatalities and injuries peaked in 2022, with a slight decline in fatalities in 2023 while injuries continued to rise marginally. Category-wise analysis shows that pedestrians and two-wheeler riders are the most vulnerable groups, having a higher proportion of fatalities compared to other vehicle types.

Vehicle-based analysis indicated that private cars, vehicles carrying goods and unidentified vehicle types contribute to the majority of crashes, whereas specialized vehicles such as ambulances and military vehicles are involved in comparatively less accidents. Trends across categories remained largely stable, with only minor fluctuations observed over the years.

Environmental and behavioral factors such as weather conditions, driver impairment and traffic signal violations were found to significantly influence accident occurrence. Higher crashes were observed during hazy and rainy conditions, while extreme conditions like storms and fog showed fewer incidents, possibly due to reduced road usage. Lighting analysis suggested that well lit dark conditions experience the highest number of crashes, likely due to increased traffic during nighttime hours.

Speed did not show a strong influence on number of fatalities, which may indicate limitations in the dataset rather than being a true statement. In contrast, behavioral factors such as driver impairment and lack of seatbelt usage were associated with higher fatality outcomes.

Time-based analysis showed peak accident occurrences during afternoon hours (15:00–16:00), aligning with increased traffic activity, while relatively fewer accidents occurred during early afternoon periods.

Overall, the project demonstrates that traffic accidents are influenced by a combination of environmental and behavioral factors. These derived insights can support data driven decision making for improving road infrastructure, enforcing safety regulations, and enhancing emergency response systems to reduce accident frequency and severity to save lives.
